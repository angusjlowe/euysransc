March 19th - 20th 2016

What is It?


Edinburgh University Young Scientific Research Association (EUYSRA) is proud to host its 3rd annual research conference for independent scientific research. The conference brings together students from around the world who are passionate about science and are considering a career in scientific research. Undergraduates and postgraduates will be given the opportunity to present their independent research using posters and other visual creations to fellow budding scientists from around the UK. The event will continue the work of EUYSRA, a student-run society for scientifically curious students wishing to broaden their horizons and gain valuable experience in original and degree independent research. Together with the staff from Edinburgh University, we are planning an inspirational programme in true Scottish tradition.

Keynote Speaker


Professor Ramamoorthy is a Reader in the School of Informatics at The University of Edinburgh, where he is affiliated with the Institute of Perception, Action and Behaviour. He is also an Executive Committee Member of the Edinburgh Centre for Robotics.

Professor Ramamoorthy's focus is on building robustly autonomous robotic systems and other decision making agents, capable of intelligently working with humans and other robots. This involves the development of new models and algorithmic techniques for (interactive) machine learning, planning and decision making, as well as practical work involving robots, wearable and networked sensors and human behavioural experiments. This is motivated by applications involving autonomous robotics, capable of actively learning and reasoning about models, and the uses of such interactively intelligent robots in homes, factories and field environments such as energy and environmental systems.

Previously, he was at the Artificial Intelligence Lab in The University of Texas at Austin. He also spent several years working with National Instruments Corp. in the control design, dynamic simulation and computer vision groups.
